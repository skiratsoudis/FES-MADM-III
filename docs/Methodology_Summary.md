# Methodology Summary

## Overview

FES-MADM III (Fuzzy Entropy-Synergy Multi-Attribute Decision-Making III) is an advanced multicriteria decision-analytics framework developed to support transparent ranking, uncertainty-aware evaluation, and robustness-oriented comparative assessment under varying operating environments.

The framework integrates fuzzy-data representation, entropy-based information extraction, weighted aggregation, and scenario-sensitive comparative diagnostics into a unified computational structure.

It is designed for decision contexts where multiple alternatives must be evaluated simultaneously across numerous criteria while external conditions may change materially.

---

## Core Methodological Philosophy

The framework is based on four central principles:

1. **Uncertainty Representation**  
   Input data may contain ambiguity, variability, or incomplete precision. Fuzzy representations are used to model such conditions in a structured manner.

2. **Information-Driven Weighting**  
   Criterion importance is not assigned arbitrarily. Entropy logic is employed to quantify discriminatory power and informational contribution.

3. **Integrated Ranking Logic**  
   Weighted multicriteria aggregation is used to synthesize criterion-level evidence into comparative alternative performance scores.

4. **Robustness Across Scenarios**  
   Rankings are examined under multiple external environments in order to identify stable, vulnerable, or scenario-sensitive alternatives.

---

## General Computational Workflow

The framework is implemented through the following sequential stages:

### Step 1 — Problem Structuring

Define:

- Alternatives to be evaluated  
- Criteria set  
- Benefit / cost orientation of criteria  
- Scenario environments

### Step 2 — Data Representation

Input observations are organized into structured matrices and represented under the selected uncertainty treatment.

### Step 3 — Normalization

Raw criterion values are transformed into dimensionless comparable scales while preserving preference direction.

### Step 4 — Entropy-Based Assessment

Criterion dispersion and informational content are evaluated to derive objective weighting signals.

### Step 5 — Integrated Weight Construction

Criterion weights are synthesized according to the adopted weighting architecture.

### Step 6 — Aggregation and Ranking

Alternative performances are aggregated into final comparative scores and ranked accordingly.

### Step 7 — Scenario Comparison

Outputs are compared across all uploaded scenarios to detect ranking shifts, resilience patterns, and environmental sensitivity.
